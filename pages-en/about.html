My Education & Experience

When I went to the bank branch with my father in middle school, the bank staff would perform transactions on the computer and tell my father the results. When I asked the bank staff how these transactions were done, they said it was something bankers could do, but what I was curious about was learning how the transactions were done behind the scenes. That's when I learned about the profession of computer engineering. After conducting detailed research among teachers, instructors, and bank employees, I learned in detail what computer engineering was and what it entailed. Teachers and instructors said that if I started my career in high school, my education and development process would be better. I decided that starting my career in high school would be more beneficial in terms of vocational learning and development. My vocational learning and development process began in the computer programming department of a vocational high school.

In high school education;

Using the knowledge I gained from my computer programming education, I developed automation applications using the Pascal and Q-Basic languages.

In associate degree education;

Using the knowledge I gained from my education in computer programming, I developed automation applications using Delphi and Visual Basic. I also created a library application using Microsoft Access.

During my undergraduate education;

Using the knowledge I gained from my education in computer systems engineering, I developed a library application using the C# language. I also developed a web application that filters videos based on age and gender, similar to YouTube.
