Education Journey

During my middle school years, I became fascinated by the computer systems used by bank employees while visiting a bank branch with my father. When I asked how those operations were performed, I was told that it was simply part of a banker’s job. However, what truly caught my attention was how the processes worked behind the scenes on the computer. This curiosity led me to develop a strong interest in computer engineering and software development.

Through conversations and research with teachers, lecturers, and industry professionals, I had the opportunity to gain a deeper understanding of the field of computer engineering. Based on the guidance I received, I learned the importance of starting professional development at an early age. For this reason, I chose the Computer Programming department at a vocational high school for my secondary education.

During the final years of my high school education, I learned that, due to the coefficient system applied to vocational high school graduates at the time, transitioning directly into a bachelor’s degree program was quite challenging. At that period, vocational high school students were generally directed toward computer teaching programs for undergraduate education. However, I wanted to continue in a field that aligned with the technical education I had received in high school and pursue an engineering degree.

After conducting extensive research, I discovered that I could first complete an associate degree and later continue my education in engineering through the Vertical Transfer Exam (DGS). Since this path aligned with my career goals, I planned my educational journey accordingly.
